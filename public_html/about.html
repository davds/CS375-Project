Conway's Game of Death

Conway's Game of Life is a simple automated cell simulation with only two rules:

  1. If a non-living cell has 2-3 living cell neighbors, it will be alive in the next “generation”.
  2. If a living cell has exactly 3 neighbors, it will stay alive in the next “generation”. If it has less than 3, it dies of “underpopulation”. If it has more, it dies of “overpopulation”.

Conway’s Game of Death is a cell-automated battle royale based on Conway’s Game of Life. The game uses the same life/death logic as CGOL, but with a competitive twist that allows players to fight for cells.

Placing Gliders

  1. While waiting for 4 players to connect to a game, players are free to place up to 3 gliders. Right click to rotate a glider, left click to place it. When you have 3 gliders placed, placing another will remove your oldest placed glider.

  2. During the placing phase, players can view the “obstacles” on the board. These are a collection of still lifes and oscillators that either stay still or infinitely loop through a repeating pattern within a bounding box.

Game Start

  • After the game starts, players are no longer allowed to place gliders.

  • The rules of CGOL will dictate which cells live and die throughout the course of the game.
  • Collisions with other players will initiate a strength check; the player with the most living cells on the board at the time of the collision will “conquer” a contested cell.
  • Players will always win collisions with obstacles. However, cells with 3 or more neighbors die of “overpopulation” so colliding with an obstacle will not always result in a player’s benefit.
  • Every 5 generations, a red zone closes in from every side of the board. Cells cannot survive inside of the red zone.
  • Throughout the course of the game, the player names on the right will be sorted based on how many living cells they own on the board.

  • The game ends when only one player has cells on the board. In the event of a tie, any players with living cells the generation before all cells “died” will be declared a winner.


Post Game

  • When the game has ended, the winner is declared for everyone in the game.

  • The stats accumulated from the game (games played, wins, total strength) will be updated on the leaderboard.
  • Return to the home page after relishing your victory (or wallowing in defeat) to play again!
